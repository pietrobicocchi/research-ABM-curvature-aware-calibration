\section{Adam learning-rate sweep}
\label{supp:adam-lr}

Adam's divergence on Brock--Hommes calibration is structural, not a hyperparameter artefact. Across $\eta \in \{10^{-1}, 10^{-2}, 10^{-3}, 10^{-4}, 10^{-5}\}$ at medium difficulty, Adam diverges for $\eta \ge 10^{-3}$ and is frozen for $\eta \le 10^{-4}$. No learning rate matches OPG-LM or well-tuned SGD on parameter recovery; the figure inline is replaced at submission from \texttt{outputs/brock\_hommes/12\_adam\_lr\_sweep.png}.

\section{Hyperparameter robustness}
\label{supp:robustness}

Adam's momentum coefficient sweep $\beta_1 \in \{0.5, 0.9, 0.95, 0.99\}$ and SGD's learning-rate sweep $\eta \in \{10^{-2}, 10^{-3}, 10^{-4}\}$ confirm the narrow optimiser claim in Section~\ref{sec:limitations}: Adam diverges at the default $\beta_1 = 0.9$; SGD only matches OPG at $\eta = 10^{-3}$; OPG-LM with default damping $\lambda_0 = 100$ is robust on both models without per-problem tuning. The figure inline is replaced at submission from \texttt{outputs/brock\_hommes/15\_hyperparam\_robustness.png}.

\section{Jacobian sensitivity versus OPG dynamic range}
\label{supp:jacobian}

The per-parameter Jacobian sensitivities of \citet{querabofarull_diffabm_2025} span approximately $5 \times 10^2$ on Brock--Hommes at the same evaluation point where the OPG eigenvalues span $5 \times 10^6$ --- a four-orders-of-magnitude gap that is the contribution of off-diagonal coupling ($|\rho(g_1, g_2)| = 0.999$, $|\rho(b_1, b_2)| = 0.96$) the per-parameter view cannot surface.
