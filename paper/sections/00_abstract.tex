\begin{abstract}
Differentiable agent-based models make calibration tractable: each gradient step already returns a buffer of per-seed gradients alongside the loss. What the calibrator does not see is that Adam will silently fail on these problems long before the budget is spent. The outer product of those same gradients --- the OPG matrix, distinct from the Fisher \citep{kunstner_opg_2019} --- is a live identifiability diagnostic: its eigendecomposition names the parameter combinations the data cannot constrain, before any update is taken. Across three structurally different models --- a financial heterogeneous-agent model, mean-field SIR, and a discrete-state network SIR with Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate gradients --- three discrepancies the calibrator never saw return stiff/sloppy ratios from $\mathbf{489\times}$ to $\mathbf{9.3 \times 10^5}$. The diagnostic predicts the direction in which Adam fails: $\mathbf{25/25}$ seeds across the financial and epidemic models diverge along the flagged sloppy combination, with cross-optimiser recovery-error ratio $\mathbf{9.4 \times 10^4}$.
\end{abstract}
