\begin{abstract}
Differentiable agent-based modelling makes gradient-based calibration tractable and hands the calibrator a buffer of per-seed gradients on every step. We show that the outer-product matrix of those gradients --- the OPG matrix, distinct from the Fisher \citep{kunstner_opg_2019} --- is a live identifiability diagnostic for non-likelihood maximum-mean-discrepancy calibration: its eigendecomposition names the parameter combinations the data cannot constrain, before any optimisation step is taken. We validate non-circularly using three discrepancies the calibrator never saw on three structurally different agent-based models (a financial heterogeneous-agent model, mean-field SIR, and a discrete-state network SIR with Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate gradients), with stiff/sloppy ratios from $\mathbf{489\times}$ to $\mathbf{9.3 \times 10^5}$. The diagnostic correctly predicts the direction of Adam's failure ($25/25$ seeds across the financial and epidemic models; cross-optimiser Adam~$v_P$~/~OPG~$v_1$ recovery-error ratio $\mathbf{9.4 \times 10^4}$).
\end{abstract}
