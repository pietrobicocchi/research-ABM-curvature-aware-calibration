\begin{abstract}
Differentiable agent-based models make calibration tractable: each gradient step already returns a buffer of per-seed gradients alongside the loss. The calibrator does not yet read it. The outer product of those same gradients --- the OPG matrix --- is a live identifiability diagnostic: its eigendecomposition names the parameter combinations the data cannot constrain, before any update is taken. We validate it non-circularly on two model families across three regimes --- a financial heterogeneous-agent model, mean-field SIR, and a discrete-state network SIR with Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate gradients --- against three discrepancies the calibrator never saw, with stiff/sloppy ratios from $\mathbf{489\times}$ to $\mathbf{9.3 \times 10^5}$ on every channel of every model. The diagnostic predicts where Adam silently fails: $25$ of $25$ runs across the financial and epidemic models diverge along the flagged sloppy combination ($95\%$ lower confidence bound $\approx 88\%$), and on a five-seed Brock--Hommes subset the cross-optimiser recovery-error ratio Adam~$v_P$~/~OPG~$v_1$ reaches $\mathbf{9.4 \times 10^4}$.
\end{abstract}
