\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We test the diagnostic against three claims: that the MMD calibration
loss has a sloppy spectrum (\S\ref{sec:results:bh-spectrum}); that
non-MMD discrepancies confirm the eigenvectors carry real
identifiability structure rather than a kernel artefact
(\S\ref{sec:results:falsification}); and that the eigenbasis predicts
the direction in which a first-order optimiser fails
(\S\ref{sec:results:predicts-adam}).
Section~\ref{sec:results:network-sir} transfers the same diagnostic to
a discrete-state network model under Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate
gradients. Source code, seeds, and figure-rendering scripts are
released at \texttt{<repo-url-here>} (anonymised at submission if
double-blind).

\subsection{The Brock--Hommes calibration loss has a sloppy spectrum}
\label{sec:results:bh-spectrum}

The MMD-calibration loss of the canonical Brock--Hommes asset-pricing
model \citep{brock_hommes_1997,brock_hommes_1998} has an OPG spectrum
spanning \textbf{8.4 orders of magnitude} with condition number
$\lambda_1 / \lambda_P = 2.4 \times 10^8$ at a near-truth evaluation
point (Figure~\ref{fig:bh-spectrum}; full gradient horizon $H = T = 200$).
The sloppiest direction $v_P$ is the parameter $\beta$ alone --- the
intensity of choice in the switching dynamics --- which the MMD loss
does not constrain. The stiffest direction $v_1$ is the symmetric-bias
combination $(b_1 + b_2)/\sqrt{2}$, which no per-parameter sensitivity
analysis can surface but the eigendecomposition reads off directly.
The data does not constrain individual parameters; it constrains
combinations.

The eigenstructure is moreover live from the first iterate, not just
at convergence. Bootstrapping the top and bottom eigenvectors along a
far-from-equilibrium calibration trajectory
($\|\theta_0 - \theta^*\| = 0.14$, $M=64$), the stiff direction stays
within $9.7^\circ$ of $v_1(\theta^*)$ at $t=0$ and the sloppy
direction within $20.3^\circ$, narrowing to $1.9^\circ$ and
$1.1^\circ$ respectively by convergence
(Figure~\ref{fig:supp-trajectory} in the appendix). The directions
along which a first-order optimiser will wander are visible before any
compute has been spent on the calibration.

\subsection{Non-circular validation across three models}
\label{sec:results:falsification}

If the OPG eigenvectors identified an MMD-kernel artefact rather than
identifiability of the model, the discrepancies of
Equation~\eqref{eq:disc} along $v_1$ and $v_P$ would be comparable.
They are not. We measure stiff/sloppy ratios on two model families
across three regimes --- Brock--Hommes financial
\citep{brock_hommes_1997}, mean-field SIR
\citep{kermack_mckendrick_1927}, and a discrete-state network SIR with
Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate gradients
\citep{maddison_concrete_2017,jang_gumbel_2017} --- against three
discrepancies the calibrator never saw: the first four moments, the
autocorrelation up to lag~20, and four tail quantiles. Every channel
on every model exceeds a $10^2$ ratio
(Figure~\ref{fig:falsification}); the smallest ratio is
$\mathbf{489\times}$ on Brock--Hommes moments and the largest
$\mathbf{9.3 \times 10^5}$ on mean-field SIR autocorrelation, with the
full $3 \times 3$ table of per-channel ratios in
Appendix~\ref{supp:falsification-table}. The MMD kernel mean embedding
does not appear anywhere in the test; the result is therefore not a
self-fulfilling consequence of the calibration loss.

\subsection{The diagnostic predicts where Adam fails}
\label{sec:results:predicts-adam}

The most informative test of the diagnostic is predictive: does the
eigenbasis identify, before the optimiser runs, the directions in
which a first-order method will fail? We decompose the recovery error
$\theta_T - \theta^*$ along the OPG eigenbasis at $\theta^*$ for three
optimisers --- Adam, OPG-preconditioned Levenberg--Marquardt, and SGD
--- across $15$ random unit-vector initialisations at far-from-equilibrium
difficulty on Brock--Hommes and $10$ at the matched difficulty on
mean-field SIR.

The median squared component per direction tracks the inverse
eigenvalue scaling: small-$\lambda_k$ directions are the directions
along which every optimiser fails, with Adam's failure systematically
larger (Figure~\ref{fig:predicts-adam}). Adam diverges from $\theta^*$
in $25/25$ runs across the two model families (95\% lower confidence
bound $\approx 88\%$). On a five-seed Brock--Hommes subset at the
matched medium difficulty $\|\theta_0 - \theta^*\| = 0.15$, the
cross-optimiser ratio Adam~$v_P$~/~OPG~$v_P$ is $34\times$ and
Adam~$v_P$~/~OPG~$v_1$ is $9.4 \times 10^4$.

This is the failure mode \citet{kunstner_opg_2019} identified for
likelihood maximisation, now visible in non-likelihood agent-based
calibration: a curvature-adaptive optimiser whose preconditioning is
misled by the gradient-noise covariance amplifies precisely the sloppy
directions the OPG eigenbasis flags. The diagnostic flags the failure
direction before the failure itself need be observed.

\subsection{The diagnostic survives discrete states under Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogates}
\label{sec:results:network-sir}

The hardest regime for the diagnostic is one where the gradients it
consumes are biased by construction. Discrete-state agent-based
simulators reparameterised through a Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate
\citep{maddison_concrete_2017,jang_gumbel_2017} are precisely that
regime: the standing concern is that the resulting gradients could
mislead any identifiability diagnostic that consumes them. We test
this directly on a network-SIR model with per-node Gumbel-Sigmoid
infection and recovery transitions ($N=250$, mean degree $6$,
$\tau_{\mathrm{Gumbel}} = 0.5$, $H = 200$).

The eigenvectors transfer. The stiff direction is dominated by the
initial-infection fraction with a contact-rate correction,
$v_1 \approx 0.89\, I_0 + 0.46\, \beta$; the sloppy direction is the
lockdown intensity, $v_P \approx 0.995\, f_{\mathrm{lock}}$ --- the
early-weak-versus-late-strong policy degeneracy a public-health
analyst would derive from the model equations
(Figure~\ref{fig:network-sir}). The same identifiability structure
surfaces under the surrogate bias as in the smooth mean-field SIR,
with the spectrum widening to \textbf{20.7 orders of magnitude} and
the non-MMD falsification ratios growing rather than shrinking
($10^4$--$10^5\times$ on every channel). Surrogate-gradient bias acts
on the magnitudes of $\widehat F$, not on the identifiability
structure it carries.
