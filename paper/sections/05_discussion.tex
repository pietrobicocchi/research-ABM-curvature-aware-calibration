\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The outer-product-of-gradients matrix is two decades old as a curvature surrogate \citep{schraudolph_sgn_2002,martens_kfac_2015}; we use it for a different purpose. The interesting reading of $\widehat F$ for differentiable agent-based calibration is not as a preconditioner that out-runs Adam, but as a name for the parameter combinations the data cannot constrain --- available before any optimisation step is taken, at the cost of the single eigendecomposition the gradients already buffer. The optimiser-replacement framing does not survive the experiment: OPG-preconditioned Levenberg-Marquardt and SGD reach indistinguishable loss floors on Brock--Hommes, and the diagnostic predicts that neither will recover $\theta^*$ along the sloppy directions. The same matrix tells us \emph{why}.

The eigenvectors recover parameter combinations a domain expert would derive from the model equations. On Brock--Hommes the stiff direction is the symmetric-bias combination $(b_1 + b_2)/\sqrt{2}$, a combination the per-parameter Jacobian sensitivities of \citet{querabofarull_diffabm_2025} cannot surface; on SIR the sloppy direction is the early-weak-versus-late-strong lockdown degeneracy $f_{\mathrm{lock}} + 0.1\, t_{\mathrm{lock}}$, a real public-health analogue of the timing--intensity confounding that any field epidemiologist will recognise after a fortnight of an outbreak. This is the evidence the diagnostic recovers real identifiability structure, not numerical artefact: structurally correct combinations fall out of $\widehat F$ without being put in.

The discrete-state network-SIR with Gumbel-Sigmoid surrogate transitions \citep{maddison_concrete_2017,jang_gumbel_2017} biases the gradients by construction, yet the eigenvectors stay qualitatively correct and the non-MMD falsification ratios grow, not shrink, under the bias. The diagnostic therefore transfers to the regime current differentiable-agent-based-modelling work treats as the hard case, suggesting that surrogate-gradient bias acts on the magnitudes of $\widehat F$ but not on the identifiability structure it identifies.
